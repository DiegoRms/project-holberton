Music

The music is, according to the traditional definition of the term, the art of organizing sensible and logically a coherent combination of sounds and silences respecting the principles fundamentals of melody, harmony and rhythm, through the intervention of complex psychoanimic processes.

Music is part of our lives almost since we are born, and for many it is considered the food of the soul. There are different types of music, and each person can vary in their tastes. The results of the research show that music is a powerful form of social expression that can reinforce stereotypes and favors the expression of one's identity.
But beyond the musical tastes, it is clear that the sensation of listening to music of our liking is unique and even indescribable. Music makes us feel incredible sensations and makes us feel different emotions: joy, ecstasy, happiness, sadness, love...

Musical style of the 80 '

The 1980s was the golden age of music. Great internationally recognized artists paid off for all tastes.
For us some of the most memorable are Michael Jackson, Madonna, U2, Prince or The Police, among many others.

The '80s, gave rise to a musical style composed of different currents: pop, alternative rock and thrash metal.
The origin of the thrash metal, is in the fantastic band Queen and its subject Stone Cold Crazy. Freddy Mercury knew how to become one of the most important figures of the 80 ', for his incredible voice and for being one of the first stars to recognize himself as a carrier of the HIV virus and to sing to death, rather to life, through songs that pierce to the bone like: "The Show Must Go On" and "Love of My Life".
For their part, the best representatives of rock and metal were: Guns N 'Roses, Bon Jovi, Mötley Crüe, Van Halen, Poison, Metallica, Aerosmith, INXS, U2, The Police and Depeche Mode, among others.
Those who opened the way to pop and alternative rock were: Lionel Richie, Madonna, Michael Jackson, Prince, George Michael, Whitney Houston, Bruce Springsteen, Phil Collins, Brian Adams, REM, Peter Gabriel and Cindy Lauper, among others.
